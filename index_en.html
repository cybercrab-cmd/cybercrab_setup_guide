Setting up smart routing in v2raytun

For other clients, this feature works by default.

1. Routing settings

In the v2raytun app, go to the "Settings" tab (gear icon) and select the "Traffic Rules" section.

2. Preset rules

In the menu that opens, go to the "Community Rules" subsection.

3. Profile installation

Scroll down the list, find the ".RU without VPN" profile and click the "Install" button next to it.

4. Activation

Go back to the "Traffic Rules" menu and toggle the top switch "Enable feature".

💡 What is this for?
Enabling this rule tells the application to automatically sort your traffic. Foreign resources (YouTube, Instagram) will go through the VPN, while local websites and banks will work directly. This eliminates the need to constantly turn the VPN on and off.

How to connect other devices

If you have the "Standard" plan (up to 3 devices), you can share the link quickly and easily without logging into Telegram again on new devices.

1. Open the link

On an already connected device, follow your subscription link (a page will open in the browser).

2. Show QR code

In the upper right corner of the screen, click on the "🔗" button.

3. Scanning

A QR code will appear on the screen. Scan it directly from the VPN application (Incy, V2Raytun, RabbitHole, etc.) on your second or third device.

💡 Important:

This QR code contains your unique key. Do not publish it in the public domain.

📺 Transferring a link to Android TV using LocalSend

Use this method so you don't have to type a long link with the remote.

Step 0. Copy the link

In the bot, send /start -> "Subscription". Find the "🔗 Connection link" block and click it to copy.

Step 1. Install LocalSend

Install LocalSend on the TV and on the smartphone from which you will transfer the link. Download: localsend.org

Step 2. Same network

Both devices must be on the same Wi-Fi network (you can share a hotspot from your phone).

Step 3. Paste the link

In the LocalSend app on your smartphone, click the "Paste" button.

Step 4. Send

Select your TV from the list of devices to send the link to.

Step 5. Receive file

On the TV, accept the file and copy the received link text.

Step 6. Activation

Open the link in the TV browser to go to the instructions OR paste it directly into V2Raytun/FLClashX, as described below.

If V2Raytun is installed on the TV

1. Add

Click "+" in the upper right corner of the screen.

2. Import

Select "Import from clipboard". Done!

If FLClashX is installed on the TV

1. Add

In the app, go to profiles and click "+ Add profile".

2. Import

Paste your subscription link from the clipboard into the URL field and click "Send".

Frequently Asked Questions

Which server should I choose?
Apple/Google/Microsoft are just types of masking. They all lead to the same fast server. If one mode is slow, simply choose another.
What does "Rule / Global / Direct" mean in the FLClashX app?

These are routing modes. They tell your VPN application exactly how to sort your internet traffic.

  • Rule / Routing — this is the "smart" mode used by default. The application itself separates the traffic according to the rules built into it: local sites (banks, government services, marketplaces) open directly through your provider, and social networks and foreign services go through the VPN. This is the most convenient mode for every day, so that nothing breaks.
  • Global / Proxy — full proxying mode. Absolutely all internet traffic on your device is forcefully sent through the VPN server abroad. Use it if you need to hide your real IP address for absolutely all sites without exception. Important: in this mode, many local banking applications may not work.
    Note: after turning on this mode, go to the "Proxy" tab and make sure that in the Global group that appears, the CyberCrab VPN (Selector) group is selected, and not DIRECT.
  • Direct — full VPN bypass mode. All traffic goes directly, bypassing the secure tunnel. In fact, the VPN stops masking your IP address and accessing resources, even if the connection itself is active. Typically, this mode is only needed by developers for network testing.
