What do "Rule / Global / Direct" mean in FLClashX?

These are routing modes. They instruct your V​P​N app on how exactly it should sort your internet traffic.

  • Rule / Routing — the "smart" mode, which is used by default. The app divides traffic based on built-in rules: local websites (banks, government services, marketplaces) open directly through your provider, while social networks and foreign services go through the V​P​N. This is the most convenient mode for everyday use to avoid breaking local services.
  • Global / Proxy — full proxy mode. Absolutely all internet traffic on your device is forced through the V​P​N server abroad. Use this if you need to hide your real IP address for all websites without exception. Important: many local banking apps may not work in this mode.
    Note: after enabling this mode, go to the "Proxy" tab and ensure that the CyberCrab V​P​N (Selector) group is selected in the Global group, not DIRECT.
  • Direct — full bypass mode. All traffic goes directly, bypassing the secure tunnel. Essentially, the V​P​N stops masking your IP address and providing access to resources, even if the connection itself is active. This mode is usually only needed by developers for network testing.
