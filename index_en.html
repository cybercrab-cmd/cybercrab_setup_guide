Smart Routing Setup for v2raytun

For other clients, this feature works by default.

1. Routing Settings

In the v2raytun app, go to the "Settings" tab (gear icon) and select "Traffic Rules".

2. Community Rules

In the opened menu, proceed to the "Community Rules" section.

3. Install Profile

Scroll through the list, find the ".RU without VPN" profile, and click the "Install" button next to it.

4. Activation

Go back to the "Traffic Rules" menu and toggle the very top switch "Enable feature".

💡 What is this for?
Enabling this rule tells the app to automatically sort your traffic. Foreign resources (YouTube, Instagram) will route through the VPN, while local websites and apps (local banks, public services) will connect directly. This removes the need to constantly toggle the VPN on and off.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which server should I choose?
Apple/Google/Microsoft are merely types of stealth camouflage. They all lead to the same high-speed server. If one mode feels laggy, simply select a different one.
What do "Rule / Global / Direct" mean in the FLClashX app?

These are routing modes. They instruct your VPN app how exactly it should sort your internet traffic.

  • Rule / Routing — This is the "smart" mode, which is used by default. The app splits your traffic automatically based on predefined rules: domestic websites (banks, public services, local marketplaces) open directly through your normal provider, while social media and foreign services route via the VPN. This is the most convenient mode for everyday use.
  • Global / Proxy — Full proxying mode. Absolutely all internet traffic on your device is forced through the remote VPN server. Use this if you need to mask your real IP address for every single website. Important: in this mode, many domestic banking apps may fail to load.
    Note: after enabling this mode, go to the "Proxy" tab and make sure that under the Global group you select CyberCrab VPN (Selector) rather than DIRECT.
  • Direct — Complete VPN bypass mode. All traffic routes normally, bypassing the secure tunnel. Effectively, the VPN stops masking your IP or unblocking content, even if the connection profile remains active. Typically, this mode is only needed by developers for network testing.
